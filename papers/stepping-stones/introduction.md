## A Constructive Philosophy

One lecture recorded by thelegendary Richard Feynman had a side discussion about "Babylonian" mathematics.  Dr. Feynman was contrasting the usual math development relvolving around axioms, lemmas, rules set down by the greats from Plato to Euler.  What comes from that is a more inventive and adaptive way to architect solutions.

This paper follows that constructive engineering style rather than an axiomatic presentation. Every operation introduced in the system is demonstrated explicitly and, whenever possible, verified by its inverse. Addition is validated by subtraction. Multiplication is validated by exact division. Coordinate mappings are validated by the inverse map. Reflection is validated by applying the reflection twice. Rather than beginning with abstract algebraic structures, we first build the arithmetic, show that it works step by step, and only then identify the mathematical structures that emerge.

This order is deliberate. A computation that can be performed, inspected, and reversed provides its own evidence. The abstract language of rings, fields, and lattices is valuable because it summarizes these demonstrated properties, not because it replaces them. Throughout this paper, abstractions are introduced only after the underlying constructions have been established.
